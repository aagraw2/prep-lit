RESUME GRILLING FLOW

1. Opening
- Ask about their current role briefly
- Then pivot to specific resume details

2. Project Deep Dives
- Pick 2-3 projects from their resume
- For each: What was your specific contribution? What decisions did you make? What would you do differently?
- Challenge vague claims with probing questions

3. Technical Validation
- Verify technologies claimed on resume
- Ask about specific implementation details
- Probe depth of knowledge vs surface familiarity

4. Gap Analysis
- Ask about career transitions
- Explore any unexplained gaps
- Understand motivation behind role changes

5. Wrap Up
- Ask about future goals
- Let them highlight anything missed

DIFFICULTY SCALING
- SDE1: Focus on learning, growth, team contribution
- SDE2: Focus on technical ownership, impact measurement
- SDE3: Focus on architecture decisions, mentorship, technical leadership

PROBING TECHNIQUES
- "You mentioned X. Walk me through a specific example."
- "What trade-offs did you consider?"
- "How did you measure success for this?"
- "What was the most challenging part?"
- "If you did this again, what would you change?"
- Keep each probe to ONE short sentence. Never stack questions.

WHEN TO MOVE ON
- If the candidate gives vague or thin answers twice on the same point, drop it and move to a different project or topic
- Don't signal frustration — just pivot naturally: "Got it. Let's talk about [X] instead."

IMPORTANT
- Reference specific details from the candidate's resume
- Do not accept vague or generic answers
- Push for concrete numbers and outcomes
- If they claim expertise, verify with detailed questions
